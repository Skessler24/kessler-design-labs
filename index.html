About Me

I'm a language nerd who designs systems.

I'm Samantha Kessler – a learning designer, artist, and language nerd who loves turning messy ideas into clear, joyful, human-centered experiences. I started my career as a speech-language pathologist, which means I'm obsessed with how people learn, communicate, and make meaning.

Now, I use that lens to design systems, tools, and stories that help people feel more capable and connected. My work ranges from district-wide intervention frameworks to movement-based literacy programs to quirky UX projects like a scheduling app for a fictional sandwich company.

I thrive in the space where structure meets creativity – where a system can be both functional and fun. At the heart of everything I make is a simple belief: design should help people feel seen, supported, and a little more delighted by the world.

Learning & Curriculum Design

I build structured, evidence-based learning experiences — from 20-week curricula to single-session instructional frameworks — that teachers can actually use.

Systems & UX Thinking

I map complex systems, find the friction, and redesign them for clarity and scalability. From intervention frameworks to scheduling apps — I think in flows.

Communication Design

Language is my first lens. I design systems and materials that communicate clearly across audiences — with accessibility, equity, and delight built in.
