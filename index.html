Engagement Architecture

    
Designing the conditions for meaningful participation.

    

      

        
Engagement architecture is the deliberate design of environments, routines, and supports that create the conditions for active participation. Rather than requiring a child to "be ready," we design systems that meet them where they are.

        
This includes structured environments, visual supports, routine-based learning opportunities, and prompting hierarchies that systematically fade toward independence.

      

      

        
      

    

    

      
Least-to-Most Prompting
Systematic prompt hierarchy that begins with minimal support and increases only as needed to foster independence.
PromptingABA

      
Errorless Learning
Structured presentation that minimizes errors during acquisition, building confidence and fluency from the start.
AcquisitionConfidence

      
Natural Environment Teaching
Embedding communication opportunities within naturally occurring routines and play-based activities.
Play-BasedNaturalistic

      
Visual Schedule Design
Creating predictable routines through visual representation to reduce anxiety and increase autonomy.
Visual SupportsSchedules

      
Sensory Regulation Supports
Environmental modifications and tools that support self-regulation as a foundation for engagement.
SensoryRegulation

      
Reinforcement Mapping
Identifying and systematically using meaningful reinforcers to sustain engagement and motivation.
MotivationBehavioral

Pediatric VPI

    
Communication support for children with velopharyngeal insufficiency.

    

      

        
What is VPI?

        
Velopharyngeal insufficiency (VPI) occurs when the velopharyngeal valve doesn't close completely during speech, allowing air to escape through the nose. This results in hypernasality, nasal air emission, and difficulty producing certain speech sounds.

        
VPI is commonly associated with cleft palate (repaired or unrepaired), but can also occur due to adenoid changes, neurological conditions, or after certain surgeries. Early identification and intervention are crucial for optimal speech outcomes.

        
A team approach — involving SLPs, surgeons, and families — is essential for comprehensive VPI management.

Caregiver FAQ

    

      

        
        
Many children with VPI achieve typical or near-typical resonance after appropriate intervention — whether through surgery, prosthetics, or targeted speech therapy. The outcome depends on the cause and severity, but significant improvement is very common with early, comprehensive care.

      

      

        
        
Therapy duration varies widely depending on the child's age, the underlying cause of VPI, and whether surgical intervention is needed. Some children make rapid progress in 3-6 months; others benefit from ongoing therapy over 1-2 years.

      

      

        
        
A speech bulb obturator is a removable dental prosthetic that helps close the velopharyngeal port during speech. It's custom-fitted by a prosthodontist and is sometimes used as an alternative to surgery, or as a temporary measure while waiting for surgery.

      

      

        
        
Absolutely. Children with VPI can and do attend regular school. They may receive speech therapy services through the school, and it's helpful to communicate with teachers about your child's needs so they can provide appropriate support in the classroom.

      

      

        
        
Share that your child has a speech difference related to VPI, and that they may sound nasal or be difficult to understand at times. Let the teacher know your child benefits from patience, encouragement, and opportunities to communicate without pressure. Provide any handouts from your SLP.

Emme App

        
Role: Lead DesignerTimeline: 6 monthsTools: Figma, React

        
Emme is a multi-modal AAC communication app designed for children aged 3–12 with complex communication needs. The app features a customizable symbol-based interface with built-in data tracking for clinicians.

        

          
Challenge
Existing AAC apps were either too complex for new users or too simplistic for growing communicators.

          
Process
Co-design sessions with 12 families, iterative prototyping, and clinical field testing across 3 schools.

          
Outcome
40% increase in spontaneous communication acts; adopted by 2 school districts as their primary AAC tool.

        

        
  • • Progressive complexity model allows growth without switching apps
  • • Built-in data visualization for IEP goal tracking
  • • Caregiver companion mode for home generalization

PBIS PAWS

        
Role: System DesignerTimeline: 4 monthsTools: Illustrator, InDesign

        
A comprehensive positive behavior support visual system designed for K-5 schools. PAWS (Prepared, Attentive, Working Hard, Safe) provides consistent behavioral expectations across all school environments.

        

          
Challenge
Inconsistent behavioral expectations and lack of visual supports across classrooms and common areas.

          
Process
Stakeholder interviews, environment audits, iterative design with teacher feedback loops.

          
Outcome
34% reduction in office discipline referrals; system adopted by all three buildings in the district.

        

        
  • • Location-specific visual matrices (classroom, hallway, cafeteria, playground)
  • • Student self-monitoring checklists
  • • Teacher implementation guide with fidelity tools

VPI Parent Guide

        
Role: Content DesignerTimeline: 3 monthsTools: Figma, InDesign

        
A comprehensive parent-facing guide for families navigating a VPI diagnosis, designed to bridge the gap between clinical jargon and caregiver understanding.

        

          
Challenge
Parents received dense clinical handouts that created more anxiety than understanding.

          
Process
Parent interviews, health literacy review, iterative plain-language writing, and visual hierarchy design.

          
Outcome
92% of parents reported feeling "much more confident" after reading the guide; adopted by the craniofacial team.

        

        
  • • 5th-grade reading level with medical accuracy
  • • Visual diagrams replacing complex anatomical text
  • • Companion FAQ card for quick reference

RTI System Redesign

        
Role: Lead DesignerTimeline: 2024Tools: Canva · Google Workspace · Figma

        
The district's existing RTI framework was fragmented, text-heavy, and difficult for teachers to implement consistently. This project reimagined the entire system as a visual, human-centered toolkit.

        

          
Challenge
An RTI system no one was using — buried in PDFs and inconsistent across buildings.

          
Process
Interviewed teachers, mapped the existing system, identified friction points, then redesigned workflows, tiered intervention guides, and communication tools.

          
Outcome
A complete visual RTI toolkit adopted district-wide, reducing confusion and improving fidelity of implementation.

        

        
  • • Plain language is a design decision
  • • Systems need visual entry points
  • • Teacher buy-in starts with clarity

        
      

    


    
    

      
5

      

      

        
Literacy in Motion

        
Role: Lead Designer & Curriculum ArchitectTimeline: 2023–2024Tools: Canva · Google Workspace · Figma

        
A full 20-week movement-based literacy curriculum integrating early literacy science, physical engagement, and multimodal learning for PreK–2nd grade students.

        

          
Challenge
How do you build foundational reading skills in young learners who can't sit still — and shouldn't have to?

          
Process
Grounded curriculum in the science of reading, mapped movement-based learning sequences, designed assessments and progress monitoring tools.

          
Outcome
A complete instructional framework that teachers could implement immediately, with built-in differentiation and accessibility.

        

        
  • • Movement IS learning
  • • Accessibility starts at curriculum design
  • • Assessment should feel like play

Bread Zeppelin Scheduling App

        
Role: UX Designer & Systems ThinkerTimeline: 2024Tools: Figma · Canva · Google Workspace

        
A UX design challenge to create a scheduling app for a fictional sandwich company — balancing usability, brand personality, and the real operational needs of shift workers and managers.

        

          
Challenge
Design an app that handles complex shift logic while feeling approachable and fun, not overwhelming.

          
Process
Mapped user flows for employees and managers, wireframed key interactions, prototyped the core scheduling experience with clear visual hierarchy.

          
Outcome
A fully prototyped scheduling app with intuitive shift management, availability tracking, and manager communication — all with Bread Zeppelin's playful personality intact.

        

        
  • • Playful ≠ unclear
  • • Constraint drives creativity
  • • Systems thinking applies everywhere
